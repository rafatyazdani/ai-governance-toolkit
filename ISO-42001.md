# ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System Standard

> Published December 2023. The first international standard specifying requirements for an AI management system (AIMS). Certifiable by accredited bodies.

---

## Overview

ISO 42001 is structured like ISO 27001 — a **Plan-Do-Check-Act management system** — applied to AI. For organizations already ISO 27001 certified, the structural familiarity significantly reduces implementation lift. Key difference from NIST AI RMF: 42001 is **certifiable**, making it a viable credential for enterprise sales, vendor due diligence, and regulatory defensibility.

**Why pursue ISO 42001:**
- Third-party assurance of AI governance maturity (B2B trust signal)
- Maps to EU AI Act conformity assessment pathways
- Complements ISO 27001 without duplicating it
- Increasingly requested in enterprise vendor security questionnaires

## High-Level Structure (HLS Clauses)

| Clause | Topic | Key Requirements |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| 4 | Context | Understand internal/external issues; stakeholders; AIMS scope |
| 5 | Leadership | Top management commitment; AI policy; roles and responsibilities |
| 6 | Planning | AI risk and opportunity assessment; objectives |
| 7 | Support | Resources; competence; awareness; communication; documentation |
| 8 | Operation | Operational planning; AI system lifecycle controls |
| 9 | Performance evaluation | Monitoring; measurement; internal audit; management review |
| 10 | Improvement | Nonconformity; corrective action; continual improvement |

## AI-Specific Annexes

ISO 42001 includes normative and informative annexes that extend the HLS with AI-specific controls.

**Annex A — Reference control objectives (normative):**

| Domain | Control Area |
|--------|-------------|

| A.3 | Internal organization |
| A.4 | Resources for AI systems |

| A.6 | AI system lifecycle |
| A.7 | Data for AI systems |
| A.8 | Information for interested parties |
| A.9 | Use of AI systems |
| A.10 | Third-party and customer relationships |

**Annex B — Implementation guidance (informative):**
Practical guidance for implementing Annex A controls — the starting point for gap assessments.

## Key Concepts

### AI Policy (Clause 5.2)
Top management must establish an AI policy that:
- Is appropriate to the organization's AI context
- Commits to satisfying applicable requirements
- Commits to continual improvement of the AIMS
- Is communicated internally
- Is available to interested parties

*Practitioner note: Don't just copy a template. The policy must reflect how your organization actually uses AI — a software company using AI in a product has different policy needs than a bank using AI for credit decisions.*

### AI System Lifecycle (Clause 8 + Annex A.6)
42001 requires controls across the full AI lifecycle:

```
Concept → Design → Development → Verification →
Deployment → Operation → Monitoring → Decommissioning
```

At each stage, controls address: data quality, model validation, human oversight design, documentation, and stakeholder communication.

### Risk and Impact Assessment (Clause 6.1 + Annex A.5)
Organizations must assess both:
- **AI-specific risks** (bias, drift, adversarial attacks, unexplainability)
- **Societal impacts** (how might the AI affect individuals and communities beyond direct users?)

## Gap Assessment Checklist

Use this as a starting point before a formal ISO 42001 audit.

**Clause 4 — Context**
- [ ] AI-related internal and external issues documented
- [ ] Interested parties and their requirements identified
- [ ] AIMS scope defined and documented

**Clause 5 — Leadership**
- [ ] AI policy approved by top management
- [ ] AI roles and responsibilities assigned
- [ ] Leadership demonstrably committed (budget, reviews, tone)

**Clause 6 — Planning**
- [ ] AI risk assessment methodology defined
- [ ] Opportunities from AI identified and assessed
- [ ] AI objectives set with measurement plans

**Clause 7 — Support**
- [ ] AI-specific competence requirements defined
- [ ] Training and awareness program for AI in place
- [ ] Documentation control process covers AI records

**Clause 8 — Operation**
- [ ] AI use case inventory current
- [ ] Lifecycle controls implemented and documented
- [ ] Data governance for AI training/operation in place

**Clause 9 — Performance Evaluation**
- [ ] KPIs for AIMS performance defined
- [ ] Internal audit schedule includes AI
- [ ] Management review agenda includes AI governance items

**Clause 10 — Improvement**
- [ ] AI incident/nonconformity process defined
- [ ] Corrective action records maintained

## ISO 42001 vs. ISO 27001 — What's Different

| Dimension | ISO 27001 | ISO 42001 |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Scope | Information security | AI management |
| Asset focus | Information assets | AI systems and their impacts |
| Risk focus | CIA triad risks | AI-specific risks + societal impacts |
| Lifecycle | Asset lifecycle | AI system lifecycle (distinct phases) |
| Third-party | Supplier security | AI supply chain (models, data, APIs) |
| Metrics | Security control effectiveness | AI trustworthiness dimensions |

**Integration path for ISO 27001-certified organizations:**
1. Extend scope statement to include AI systems
2. Map existing Annex A controls to 42001 Annex A (significant overlap)
3. Add AI-specific controls (A.5, A.6, A.7 have no 27001 equivalent)
4. Augment management review and internal audit to include AI topics
5. Conduct integrated audit (many certification bodies now offer joint 27001 + 42001 audits)
